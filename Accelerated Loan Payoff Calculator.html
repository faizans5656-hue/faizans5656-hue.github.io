Accelerated Loan Payoff Calculator: Visualize Savings from Extra Payments

Understanding how loans work and how you can save money through strategic payments is crucial for financial health. An amortization schedule is a detailed table that breaks down each payment on a loan, showing how much goes toward interest and how much reduces the principal balance. In the early years of a loan, the majority of each payment goes toward interest, with only a small portion reducing the principal. As time progresses, this ratio shifts, with more of each payment going toward principal reduction.

Making extra payments toward the principal can dramatically change this dynamic. When you pay extra, you reduce the principal balance faster, which in turn reduces the amount of interest that accrues in subsequent months. This creates a snowball effect where more of your regular payments go toward principal reduction rather than interest, accelerating your path to becoming debt-free.
